Read: the June heatwave (built from ~19 Jun, peaked 24–25 Jun with a Met Office Red extreme-heat warning, ~34–36°C in SE England, and continuing through 26 Jun) tracks a clear FTD slowdown. Day-of-week adjusted, FTDs ran -12.4% below normal across the 19–26 Jun heatwave and -16.9% on the peak-heat days. Spend held roughly normal, so CPA rose: heat hit conversion, not budget. 26 Jun is the cleanest data point: it was a Friday and in the late-month payday window — both of which normally lift FTDs — yet volume came in -11.2% below the Friday norm while temperatures stayed ~28°C. With the usual seasonal/payday tailwinds working against the signal, that strengthens the case that heat is suppressing sign-ups. Correlation of approx temperature with the day-of-week-adjusted FTD index is -0.54 (strong negative); with PLTV/FTD only 0.41, so players who do sign up in the heat are of broadly normal value — it's a volume effect. Caveats: temperature is an approximation reconstructed from Met Office reporting (same source as the prior build), not a measured daily feed — and the 26 Jun value was corrected to ~28°C/heatwave after an earlier build wrongly assumed the heat had broken; swap in an exact series for precision. FTD counts are reliable but recent PLTV/FTD points are the least-matured cohort and revise up; 26 Jun spend is understated by the affiliate feed lag. Correlation ≠ causation — school terms, holidays and major sport can co-move with hot spells.
