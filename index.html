Timing & payday effects (2026 YTD)

Weekends and month-end carry the highest daily FTD volume (peak around day 30 — the late-month payday window). Early-week (Mon–Wed) and early-month days are systematically lowest, which is why the this-week forecast never linearly scales a part-week by 7/days-landed.

Day-of-week adjusted, FTDs ran -8.6% below normal across the 19–28 Jun heatwave and -10.4% on the peak-heat days (24–28 Jun). Spend held roughly normal, so CPA rose: heat hit conversion, not budget. The 27–28 Jun weekend — normally a volume lift — came in -3.1% below the weekend norm while temperatures stayed ~27–29°C, reinforcing the signal. Correlation of approx temperature with the day-of-week-adjusted FTD index is -0.54 (negative); with PLTV/FTD only 0.50, so players who do sign up in the heat are of broadly normal value — it is a volume effect. Caveats: temperature is an approximation reconstructed from Met Office reporting (27 Jun ≈ 29°C, 28 Jun ≈ 27°C this run), not a measured daily feed — swap in an exact series for precision. FTD counts are reliable but the most recent PLTV/FTD points are the least-matured cohort and revise up; 28 Jun spend is understated by the affiliate-feed lag. Correlation ≠ causation — school terms, holidays and major sport can co-move with hot spells.
