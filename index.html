What drove the CPA drop — spend vs volume (YoY bridge)

CPA = spend ÷ FTDs. Bridging 2025 → 2026: spend grew +39% which on its own would raise CPA by +£66, but FTDs grew +73% which cuts CPA by £-100. Net £-34 (£171 → £137, -19.8%). The paid-vs-non-paid FTD mix is broadly unchanged YoY (~44% non-paid), so this is leverage, not a shift to organic.

ATL impact model — Jan–Jun 2026 (brand/TV halo · scenario model)

How to read this. ATL (TV, radio, AVOOH, online video, sponsorship) is always-on and near-flat week to week, and carries no directly-attributed FTDs. With no on/off or geo variation in the data, a regression cannot causally identify ATL's effect — fitting weekly halo demand on adstocked ATL spend returns a near-zero/negative coefficient dominated by trend and other paid spend. So this is a transparent benchmark-anchored scenario model, not a fitted causal estimate: we attribute a plausible halo share of demand in the channels ATL lifts (Direct, Organic Search, brand PPC, RAF, iOS/Android Organic, Unattributed, Referral, Organic Social) and size the money. Treat as directional; the only way to measure this properly is a regional hold-out or a deliberate spend on/off test (see recommendation).

TV is 83% of ATL spend and carries most of the halo. 10s spots cost ~23% less per incremental FTD (£548 vs £714) — airtime scales with duration but a 10s still delivers ~65% of the response. Tilt acquisition-weighted bursts toward 10s; keep 30s for brand-building.
  2. Weight ATL to amplify brand search, Direct & Organic — and don't cap those channels while TV is live. ~62% of the modelled halo lands in PPC Brand, Organic Search and Direct. The cheapest way to waste TV is to let brand-search budgets or bid caps throttle the demand it creates — uncap brand PPC during heavy TV weeks so the lift is captured, not lost.
  3. Concentrate the long tail, don't spread it. Sponsorship, Online Video, AVOOH, OOH and the LED/partnership placements (SportFIVE, QPR, Bournemouth, DAZN, Ladbible…) are each 0–3% of spend and individually unmeasurable. Consolidate into 2–3 properties you can actually read in a test rather than a thin spread across a dozen.
  4. Size the budget against multi-year LTV, not 12-month. On 12-month net PLTV alone ATL returns ~0.21x and would need to drive ~62.2% of all halo-channel FTDs just to break even — it won't on that lens. Its case rests on longer LTV, cheaper brand search and category presence, so defend the budget on brand/efficiency grounds and judge it there, not on last-click CPA.
  5. Ring-fence ~10–15% for a clean measurement test — the highest-value action. Run a regional TV hold-out (dark 1–2 regions for 6–8 weeks) or a national on/off burst and read the halo-channel + brand-search response. That single test turns every number on this tab from scenario into measured, and lets you rank media types — and eventually specific properties — for the first time.
