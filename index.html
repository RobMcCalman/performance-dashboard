What drove the CPA drop — spend vs volume (YoY bridge)

CPA = spend ÷ FTDs. Bridging 2025 → 2026: spend grew +37% which on its own would raise CPA by +£64, but FTDs grew +72% which cuts CPA by £-98. Net £-35 (£171 → £136, -20.4%). The volume effect dominates — FTDs grew about twice as fast as spend, with fixed brand/ATL investment (~£10.13m YTD, 0 attributed FTDs) amortised across a much larger base. The paid-vs-non-paid FTD mix is broadly unchanged YoY (~44% non-paid), so this is leverage, not a shift to organic.

Timing & payday effects (2026 YTD)

Weekends and month-end carry the highest daily FTD volume (peak around day 30 — the late-month payday window). Early-week (Mon–Wed) and early-month days are systematically lowest, which is why the this-week forecast never linearly scales a part-week by 7/days-landed.

Read: the June heatwave (built from ~19 Jun, peaked 24–27 Jun with a Met Office Red Extreme Heat warning, ~36–38°C highs in SE England, easing from 28 Jun) tracks a clear FTD slowdown. Day-of-week adjusted, FTDs ran -5.5% below normal across the 19 Jun–1 Jul heatwave and -13.1% on the peak-heat days (24–27 Jun). Spend held roughly normal, so CPA rose: heat hit conversion, not budget. The 27–28 Jun weekend — normally a volume lift — came in -3.1% below the weekend norm while temperatures stayed ~27–29°C, reinforcing the signal. Correlation of approx temperature with the day-of-week-adjusted FTD index is -0.51 (negative); with PLTV/FTD only 0.53, so players who do sign up in the heat are of broadly normal value — it is a volume effect. Caveats: temperature is an approximation reconstructed from Met Office reporting (27 Jun ≈ 29°C, 28 Jun ≈ 27°C, 29 Jun ≈ 23°C, 30 Jun ≈ 21°C this run — hot spell broken), not a measured daily feed — swap in an exact series for precision. FTD counts are reliable but the most recent PLTV/FTD points are the least-matured cohort and revise up; the latest day's spend is understated by the affiliate-feed lag. Correlation ≠ causation — school terms, holidays and major sport can co-move with hot spells.

Insights

What the data is saying

  1. Acquisition is well ahead of last year — YTD FTDs +72% vs 2025 at -20.4% lower CPA. Blended LTV:CAC 1.10 (net) vs 0.99 (gross) last year.
  2. June is pacing slightly behind plan — PLTV 94% and FTDs 96% of target. The gap is value-mix, not volume: blended PLTV/FTD £146 vs May £153.
  3. Direct is the June dilutant — 4.6% of FTDs at £53/FTD. Premium channels (Organic Search, PPC Generic) held value but lost share.
  4. Best-returning paid channel: Apple Ads Brand at 1.67 net LTV:CAC. Brand search and UAC ad groups top the leaderboard.
  5. Affiliate is the largest paid channel (33.4% of June FTDs) and the softest premium spend — 4 large affiliate(s) below 0.8 net this 4-week window.
  6. Quality holding: blended cost per APD2+ £286 YTD; value-per-FTD is stable week to week, so FTD volume — not per-player value — drives PLTV.

APD2+ rate vs value — by channel (YTD)

Each bubble = a channel (size = FTD volume). Day-2 activation rate (x) and net PLTV per FTD (y) are strongly correlated across channels (r ≈ 0.86) — APD2+ is a good leading proxy for value.
