What drove the CPA drop — spend vs volume (YoY bridge)

CPA = spend ÷ FTDs. Bridging 2025 → 2026: spend grew +38% which on its own would raise CPA by +£65, but FTDs grew +72% which cuts CPA by £-99. Net £-34 (£171 → £137, -20.0%). The volume effect dominates — FTDs grew about twice as fast as spend, with fixed brand/ATL investment (~£10.45m YTD, 0 attributed FTDs) amortised across a much larger base. The paid-vs-non-paid FTD mix is broadly unchanged YoY (~44% non-paid), so this is leverage, not a shift to organic.
