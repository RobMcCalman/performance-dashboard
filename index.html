4b · Timing & payday effects

FTDs by day of month

avg per day, Jan–May 2026 · yellow = month-end payday window (27th+); line = PLTV/FTD

FTDs by day of week

avg per day, Jan–May 2026 · pink = Friday

Payday is a real, recurring driver. The last days of every month (≈27th–month-end) consistently pull 5% more FTDs than mid-month (~1,207/day vs ~1,148) and 20% more than early-month, at a higher value per player (PLTV/FTD ~£166 vs ~£144 early-month). It shows as more depositors, not bigger deposits (first-deposit size is flat at ~£22). Layered on top, Friday is the strongest weekday (~1,391 FTDs vs ~994 Mon–Wed). So end-of-month surges like 29 May (a Friday in the payday window, in Spring-Bank-Holiday week) are payday + day-of-week + holiday stacking — not a trend break — and they inflate that month's comparison base.

5 · Insights

  • Volume is well ahead of last year — YTD FTDs are up ~+72.4% vs 2025 on the hybrid baseline, while CPA is down ~20% and LTV:CAC up ~13%. Growth is coming with improving efficiency, not just more spend.
  • Organic & app installs carry the value — Organic Search, iOS Organic and RAF deliver the highest LTV:CAC and most of the non-paid FTDs; App platform LTV:CAC (2.4) is inflated by organic installs attributed to App.
  • Affiliate is the swing factor — at £1.79M/4wk it is the largest controllable line and sits right at breakeven (1.04). On actual per-affiliate PLTV the worst large partners are IDs 2014 (0.72) and 2098 (0.66); the two biggest spenders (2164 0.84, 2630 0.90) are closer to breakeven than the earlier estimate implied.
  • Meta is the clearest efficiency problem — Meta Paid Social (0.78) and the iOS install ad sets in Meta App (0.38–0.45) are underwater on predicted LTV; reallocating from them is the cleanest budget-neutral win.
  • ATL remains unmeasured in last-click — £1.70M/4wk with zero attributed FTDs; the only honest read is the £48.04 blended implied cost per total FTD, which argues for a proper geo-lift / MMM test.
